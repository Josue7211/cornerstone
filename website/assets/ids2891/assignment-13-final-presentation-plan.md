## Deliverables

### 1. Interactive Website: "From Pixels to Intelligence"
A scroll-driven, multimodal website that walks visitors through the evolution of AI hardware architecture. Built with AI-assisted development (vibe coded), demonstrating the very technology being researched.

**Sections:**
1. **Hero/Hook** — "Every AI tool you use runs on hardware designed to play video games"
2. **The CPU Problem** — Animated visualization of sequential vs parallel processing
3. **GPU Revolution Timeline** — Interactive timeline from GeForce 256 (1999) → CUDA (2007) → AlexNet (2012) → Tensor Cores (2017) → Blackwell (2024)
4. **How GPUs Actually Work** — Animated data flow showing thousands of cores processing in parallel
5. **Specialized Chips** — Side-by-side animated comparison: CPU vs GPU vs TPU vs NPU data flow
6. **The Numbers** — Market stats, performance benchmarks, growth projections
7. **What This Means** — Future implications, accessibility, who controls AI infrastructure
8. **Sources & Further Reading** — Full bibliography with links

**Tech stack:** HTML/CSS/JS with GSAP animations, scroll-triggered sections. Single-page, no backend needed. Hosted on GitHub Pages or similar for permanent URL.

### 2. Concise Research Paper
A focused research paper (2,000–3,000 words) synthesizing findings on AI hardware architecture evolution. Follows the structure of the AutoResearchClaw output format:

- **Introduction** — Context and research questions
- **Background / Related Work** — History of GPU computing, CUDA, prior surveys
- **Comparative Analysis** — CPU vs GPU vs TPU vs NPU architectures (the core contribution)
- **Future Implications** — Market trends, accessibility, emerging architectures
- **Conclusion** — Summary and advocacy for hardware literacy
- **References** — Full APA bibliography (13+ sources)

## Why This Format
- **Not an essay** — The assignment explicitly encourages creative modalities
- **Not PowerPoint** — The website IS the visual component AND the research artifact
- **Multimodal** — Combines text, animation, interactive elements, and data visualization
- **Accessible** — 99% of the class is non-technical; interactive visuals > academic prose
- **Permanent** — A live URL is a shareable, lasting artifact
- **Meta-demonstrative** — Building the website with AI proves the thesis about AI hardware's transformative power
