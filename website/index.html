Architecture comparison

Why GPUs Beat CPUs at AI

A CPU uses a few powerful cores for complex tasks. A GPU uses thousands of tiny cores for simple, parallel math. Neural networks need the same calculation done millions of times — exactly what GPUs were built for.

2007 — The turning point

CUDA Changed Everything

"CUDA was not a product feature. It was a platform moat."

When NVIDIA released CUDA in 2007, it made GPU hardware programmable for the first time. Jensen Huang invested hundreds of millions in a software platform with no immediate revenue. The bet was strategic: if NVIDIA owned the software layer, every developer who adopted CUDA would be locked into NVIDIA hardware forever.

Five years later, AlexNet proved him right. Two $500 gaming GPUs (GTX 580s) trained a neural network that crushed the competition by 11 percentage points — a result that pivoted the entire AI research community to GPU computing overnight.

CRV Science, 2024 · Krizhevsky et al., 2012

2010 – 2024

The Architecture Arms Race

Each NVIDIA generation became more deliberately optimized for AI. What started as an accident became a trillion-dollar strategy.

2010

Fermi

ECC memory and double-precision made GPUs viable for scientific computing — the first step beyond gaming.

2012

Kepler

Dynamic parallelism — GPU programs could launch GPU programs. AlexNet ran on this generation.

AlexNet moment
2016

Pascal

NVLink interconnect enabled multi-GPU training — essential as models grew beyond single-card memory.

2017

Volta

Introduced Tensor Cores — dedicated matrix multiplication hardware. GPUs stopped being "graphics chips repurposed for AI" and became "AI chips that also do graphics."

Inflection point
2020

Ampere

Sparsity-aware Tensor Cores doubled throughput by skipping zero-valued computations in pruned networks.

2022

Hopper

The Transformer Engine — hardware built specifically for the attention mechanism powering GPT, Claude, and every modern LLM.

2024

Blackwell

NVLink 5 at 1.8 TB/s bandwidth. Second-gen Transformer Engines for trillion-parameter models. The data center GPU.

Bitdeer, 2025 · Rathore, 2024 · Data Center Knowledge, 2025

Beyond the GPU

The Challengers: Purpose-Built AI Silicon

TPU

Google's Tensor Processing Unit

Uses systolic arrays instead of general-purpose cores. Data flows through processing elements in waves — no memory bottleneck. 15–30x better performance per watt than GPUs on inference.

4.6 petaflops per chip (v7 Ironwood)
Cloud only — you can't buy one.
NPU

Neural Processing Units

Embedded in every new iPhone, every Copilot+ PC, every Snapdragon phone. When Apple, Microsoft, and Qualcomm all embed AI hardware in consumer devices — it's not a trend. It's a verdict.

80 TOPS — Snapdragon X2 NPU
Already in your pocket.
M-series

Apple Silicon

CPU + GPU + Neural Engine on one die with unified memory. No copying data between processors. A MacBook with 24GB unified memory runs AI models that choke on traditional laptops with 4GB VRAM.

15.8 TOPS — Neural Engine
Privacy by design.

The real revolution

Software Is Eating Hardware

The most important AI advances of the past two years haven't been in hardware. They've been in software that makes existing chips dramatically more capable.

10×

Model Compression

Quantization shrinks a 140GB model to 35GB. A 70B-parameter model fits on a consumer GPU. The hardware didn't change — the math got smarter.

2–4×

Token Optimization

PagedAttention cuts memory waste by 50%. Flash Attention restructures computations to keep data in fast on-chip SRAM. Speculative decoding doubles generation speed.

∞

Open-Source Models

Meta's Llama, Mistral, Qwen, DeepSeek — free, downloadable, competitive with proprietary models. The quality gap has narrowed from years to months.

Meta AI, 2024 · Sanmartín & Prohaska, 2023

The human barrier

The Psychology of Cloud Dependence

🏦

The Bank

Cloud AI offers brand recognition, professional interfaces, and someone to blame when things go wrong. You're paying for the comfort of delegation — not better results.

Subscription
🛏️

The Mattress

Local AI is less convenient and demands more responsibility. But your data never leaves your machine. The bank can be robbed. The mattress can't.

Ownership
"Cloud AI feels like a subscription.
Local AI feels like a workshop."

The Delegation Premium

People pay more for outcomes they could achieve themselves — because delegating reduces the anxiety of being personally responsible for failures.

The Legitimacy Illusion

Millions pay $20/month for ChatGPT when free alternatives match its performance on most tasks. "Free" feels inferior — even when it isn't.

The FOMO Treadmill

GPT-4 → GPT-4 Turbo → GPT-4o → o1 → o3. Each version creates the impression you'll fall behind without the latest subscription. The performance gap between versions is often marginal.

The architects

Pioneers of AI Hardware

The people whose decisions, inventions, and bets shaped the hardware powering artificial intelligence.

JH

Jensen Huang

CEO, NVIDIA

Bet hundreds of millions on CUDA when GPUs were just for gaming. Created the platform moat that made NVIDIA the engine of AI.

GH

Geoffrey Hinton

Nobel Laureate · Deep Learning Pioneer

Spent decades advocating for neural networks when the field abandoned them. His student's AlexNet proved GPUs could think. Won the Nobel Prize for foundational ML work.

GM

Gordon Moore

Co-founder, Intel

His 1965 observation — transistor density doubles every two years — drove 50 years of exponential progress. When Moore's Law stalled, specialized AI chips became necessary.

LS

Lisa Su

CEO, AMD

Transformed AMD from near-bankruptcy into NVIDIA's only serious GPU competitor. AMD's MI300X challenges NVIDIA's data center dominance.

FL

Fei-Fei Li

Stanford · ImageNet Creator

Built ImageNet — 14 million labeled images that proved AI needs not just hardware and algorithms, but painstakingly curated data. AlexNet won on her benchmark.

AK

Andrej Karpathy

Former Head of AI, Tesla

One of the most influential voices in AI democratization. His open-source contributions and public teaching accelerated the shift from cloud to local AI.

DA

Dario Amodei

CEO, Anthropic

Publicly acknowledged the tension: safety arguments for closed models conflict with the reality that open models are rapidly closing the capability gap.

JK

Jim Keller

Chip Architect

Designed transformative chips at AMD (Zen), Apple (A-series), Tesla (FSD chip), and Intel. One of the most prolific silicon architects in history.

SA

Sam Altman

CEO, OpenAI

Argues that AI risks justify keeping it centralized. The counter-argument: centralized control creates its own dangers — single points of failure, opaque decision-making, concentrated power.

The thesis

AI Is Not a Service to Be Rented

💻

This paper was researched, drafted, and fact-checked with AI models running on an $800 consumer GPU — an NVIDIA RTX 4070 Ti SUPER in a desktop I built myself.

🔒

My data never left my machine. No API fees. No rate limits. No terms of service. Through Ollama, I run quantized Llama models at conversational speeds.

🌐

By 2031, fully capable AI assistants will operate entirely on-device — no internet required. The 2.6 billion people without reliable internet could finally access AI.

The cloud-first era of AI was not a destination. It was a stopover — a necessary phase that existed because consumer hardware couldn't keep up. That constraint is dissolving.

AI is a tool to be owned.

"The generation that grows up building homelabs, running open-source models, and treating AI as infrastructure they control — will be the generation that determines what this technology becomes."
