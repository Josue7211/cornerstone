<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Document Structure
- **D-01:** Process-narrative structure organized chronologically: topic selection → source gathering → analysis methodology → key findings → implications
- **D-02:** Sections should flow as a research journal/narrative, not as a formal literature review
- **D-03:** 750+ words minimum — aim for 800-900 to provide adequate depth without padding

### Content Focus
- **D-04:** Hybrid approach — document both the research process AND key findings
- **D-05:** Emphasize the "journey" aspect: how initial interest in AI hardware evolved into the specific democratization/local AI angle
- **D-06:** Include personal connection (homelab, desktop PC, RTX 4070 Ti SUPER) as motivation for research direction
- **D-07:** Explain how the three research questions emerged from background research
- **D-08:** Discuss interdisciplinary connections (computer science, engineering, accessibility/equity)

### Source Integration
- **D-09:** Reuse and expand the existing 13-source bibliography from check-in 3 — all verified real
- **D-10:** Sources must be woven throughout the narrative (in-text citations), not just listed at the end
- **D-11:** Minimum 10 sources cited in-text; target all 13 from existing bibliography
- **D-12:** APA 7th edition — locked from Phase 1

### Tone and Voice
- **D-13:** Academic but accessible, first-person reflective — consistent with check-in 3 and short proposal
- **D-14:** 99% non-technical audience — avoid unexplained jargon
- **D-15:** Personal and authentic — this gets run through grubby.ai by user for humanization

### Claude's Discretion
- Exact section headings and word distribution across sections
- Which sources to emphasize most heavily in the narrative
- How much detail to give on methodology vs. findings
- Transition language between sections

</decisions>
