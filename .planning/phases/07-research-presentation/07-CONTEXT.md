### Slide Structure (10-12 min)
- Slide 1: Title + hook (~30s)
- Slide 2: Research questions (~1 min)
- Slide 3-4: GPU evolution timeline (~2 min)
- Slide 5-6: How GPUs work — CPU vs GPU parallel processing diagram (~2 min)
- Slide 7-8: Specialized chips — CPU vs GPU vs TPU vs NPU comparison (~2 min)
- Slide 9: Local AI & affordability — the democratization angle (~1.5 min)
- Slide 10: Transdisciplinary connections (~1 min)
- Slide 11: Future implications & advocacy (~1 min)
- Slide 12: Sources & Q&A (~1 min)

### Visual Diagrams (Excalidraw)
1. CPU vs GPU architecture — sequential vs parallel data flow
2. GPU Evolution Timeline — GeForce 256 → CUDA → AlexNet → Tensor Cores → Blackwell
3. Chip Comparison — CPU vs GPU vs TPU vs NPU side-by-side
4. Local AI ecosystem — consumer hardware running AI models

### Research Focus
- Local AI + Affordability (democratization angle)
- Meta-narrative: AI was used to help create this presentation about AI hardware
- 99% non-technical audience — visual explanations, no unexplained jargon

### Claude's Discretion
- Exact slide count and content balance
- Diagram visual style and level of detail
- Which stats/numbers to highlight
- Speaker notes tone (conversational but informed)
