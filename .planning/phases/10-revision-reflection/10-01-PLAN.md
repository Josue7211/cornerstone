**Element A: Project Thesis (approx. 150-200 words)**
Heading: `## Project Thesis`

State the thesis clearly: The cloud-first era of artificial intelligence is a temporary phase in computing history, not its conclusion. Four converging trends — silicon miniaturization, model compression, token optimization, and open-source model availability — are making centralized AI infrastructure unnecessary for most use cases. Within five years, the default way most people interact with AI will be through models running locally on their own devices.

Then explain HOW the research led to this thesis: Started with a narrow hardware question (how did GPUs become AI accelerators?). Background research through FSW libraries revealed the democratization angle — not just what the hardware does, but who gets to use it. The personal connection — building a desktop PC with an RTX 4070 Ti SUPER and running AI models locally through Ollama — transformed the thesis from academic to experiential. The research expanded from pure hardware analysis into psychology (why people trust cloud over local), geopolitics (US-China chip export controls), economics (AI chip market projections, UBI implications), and the trajectory toward artificial general intelligence.

Cite: (Jouppi et al., 2017; Data Center Knowledge, 2025; CRV Science, 2024)

**Element B: Research Process Evolution (approx. 250-300 words)**
Heading: `## How the Research Evolved`

Discuss evolution across three dimensions:

**Scope:** The most dramatic shift was scope expansion. What started as a focused technical analysis of GPU architecture became a 13,000+ word interdisciplinary paper covering hardware evolution, software optimization, open-source ecosystems, psychology of cloud dependence, corporate behavioral analysis, geopolitics of semiconductor supply chains, military ethics, cybersecurity implications, and the trajectory toward AGI. The paper grew far beyond any course assignment — not because of requirement creep, but because the connections between these threads were genuinely inseparable. You cannot explain why local AI matters without explaining the psychology of why people resist it, and you cannot explain that psychology without understanding the corporate incentive structures that engineer cloud dependence.

**Approach/Perspective:** The critical lens shifted fundamentally. The Research Documentation assignment described a technical analysis with democratization as a secondary theme. Through deeper research, democratization became THE lens. The most surprising discovery was the psychology section — the "delegation premium" concept and the "bank versus mattress" analogy for cloud trust emerged from behavioral economics research that the initial technical framing would never have surfaced. Understanding WHY people choose cloud over local proved more interesting than understanding HOW local AI works.

**Context:** The scholarly conversation expanded from computer architecture journals into science and technology studies (STS), behavioral economics, and geopolitics. The audience broadened from engineering students to anyone whose life is affected by AI — which, increasingly, is everyone. The decision to build an interactive website rather than write an essay reflected this shift: the subject demands visual communication, and the meta-narrative (using AI to build the website that explains AI) resonates with the thesis.

Cite: (IBM, 2024; Meta AI, 2024; Rathore, 2024; Sanmartín & Prohaska, 2023)

**Element C: Strategies (approx. 150-200 words)**
Heading: `## Research Strategies and Their Effectiveness`

Discuss strategies used and evaluate them:

**Source layering strategy:** Started with tertiary sources from Credo Reference (Glassner 2012, Daintith & Wright 2008, Marcovitz 2018) for foundational vocabulary, then moved to peer-reviewed papers (Jouppi et al. 2017 was the single most important source), then supplemented with current industry analysis. This layered approach was highly effective — it built understanding incrementally and ensured the bibliography had both scholarly depth and current relevance.

**AI-assisted research:** Used locally hosted AI models (via Ollama on the RTX 4070 Ti SUPER) for drafting, fact-checking, and literature discovery. This strategy was both effective and ironic — the tools validating the thesis were themselves the thesis in practice. The experience of running AI locally, seeing what consumer hardware could do, directly informed the paper's arguments about democratization.

**What could improve:** The scope expansion, while intellectually rewarding, created a paper that far exceeded any assignment requirement. A more disciplined researcher would have constrained scope earlier. But the connections between hardware, psychology, economics, and geopolitics were too interconnected to artificially separate — the paper needed to be comprehensive to be honest.

Cite: (Glassner, 2012; Daintith & Wright, 2008; Bitdeer, 2025)
