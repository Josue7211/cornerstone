# IDS2891 Cornerstone — Spring 2026

## Overview
Interdisciplinary Studies capstone course at Florida SouthWestern State College (FSW). Semester-long research project culminating in a 10-12 minute presentation.

## Research Topic
**The History of AI Hardware Architecture: GPU and Specialized Chip Development for Image Processing Up to AI Acceleration and What This Means for Computing Going Forward.**

Central interest: Computer engineering — how hardware is designed and optimized for AI applications.

## Research Questions
1. How has the GPU architecture evolved from graphics rendering device to AI accelerator, and what are the specific design features that facilitated this evolution?
2. What is the difference from a general purpose processor (CPU) to AI specialized hardware (GPUs, TPUs, NPUs) at a basic level?
3. What does AI specialized hardware mean for the future of computing and future access to AI?

## Modes of Analysis
- **Change-over-time:** GPU architecture from gaming to AI acceleration
- **Compare/contrast:** CPU vs GPU vs TPU/NPU architectures
- **Cause & effect:** AI workload needs that justified specialized hardware

## Intended Audience
Computer engineering students, tech enthusiasts, and anyone curious about how hardware enables modern AI. Accessible without dumbing down the nuances.

## Project Type
Research findings with visual schematics showing hardware architecture evolution. Possibly video format.

## Citation Style
APA (standard for tech/engineering — user can change)

## Key Concepts
