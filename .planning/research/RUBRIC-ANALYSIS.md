## 1. The Capstone Rubric — Five Criteria

The final project (#17), final presentation (#13), co-curricular reflection (#14), research documentation (#15), and revision & reflection (#16) are ALL graded on the same Capstone Rubric with five criteria: **Design, Prepare, Create, Communicate, Reflect**.

The rubric uses a four-level scale: **Capstone > Accomplished > Developing > Beginning**. The goal is Capstone on all five.

### 1.1 Design (Capstone Level)

**What it requires:** Demonstrates sophisticated understanding of the research problem. Defines a clear, narrowed research question with justified scope. Shows evidence of deliberate design choices — why this medium, why this audience, why this approach. The project is intentionally structured, not just assembled.

**Accomplished (one step below):** Adequate understanding, reasonable scope, but design choices are conventional or insufficiently justified.

### 1.2 Prepare (Capstone Level)

**What it requires:** Thorough background research using FSW library resources (primary, secondary, tertiary sources). Multiple modes of analysis applied. Research process is documented and systematic. Sources are evaluated critically, not just collected. Bibliography exceeds minimum requirements (10+ sources, ideally many more).

**Accomplished:** Adequate sources, meets minimums, but research process is not deeply reflective or doesn't demonstrate critical evaluation of sources.

### 1.3 Create (Capstone Level)

**What it requires:** The final artifact synthesizes research with the student's own ideas. Demonstrates original analysis, not just reporting. The medium/genre is suited to the audience and enhances the argument. At least 5 sources integrated. Shows evidence of integration, accuracy, fairness, relevance, clarity, and precision.

**Accomplished:** Adequate synthesis, meets source requirements, but the artifact doesn't push beyond conventional formats or doesn't fully integrate the student's own perspective.

### 1.4 Communicate (Capstone Level)

**What it requires:** Clear, effective oral presentation (10-12 minutes). Introduces and summarizes the project. Explains methods, perspective, and scope. Makes transdisciplinary connections. States implications and conclusions. Advocates for the importance of the subject. Includes a visual component (NOT just PowerPoint). Presentation is engaging, well-paced, and demonstrates command of the material.

**Accomplished:** Adequate presentation, covers required elements, but delivery is unpolished or transdisciplinary connections feel forced rather than organic.

### 1.5 Reflect (Capstone Level)

**What it requires:** Deep, honest reflection on the research process — what changed, what surprised, what was learned. Demonstrates intellectual growth. Connects research to broader questions. Acknowledges limitations and identifies future directions. Shows awareness of how GE courses and personal background shaped the research.

**Accomplished:** Surface-level reflection, acknowledges what was done but doesn't interrogate the process or demonstrate genuine intellectual growth.
